Privacy Policy

Last Updated: June 15, 2023

At Dropleaf Pro, we are committed to protecting your privacy and ensuring the security of your personal information. This Privacy Policy outlines how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you visit our website or use our services. By accessing or using our services, you agree to the terms of this Privacy Policy.

1. Information We Collect

We collect various types of information to provide and improve our services. This includes personal information such as name, email address, phone number, and billing information when you create an account, make a purchase, or contact us. We also automatically collect technical information about your device, browsing actions, and patterns when you interact with our website.

2. How We Use Your Information

The information we collect is used to provide, maintain, and improve our services; process transactions and send you related information; respond to your comments and questions; send administrative information like policies; personalize your experience; analyze usage trends to improve our website; prevent fraudulent activities; and comply with legal obligations.

3. Information Sharing and Disclosure

We may share your information with trusted third-party service providers who perform services on our behalf, such as payment processing, data analysis, email delivery, hosting services, and customer service. We may also disclose information when required by law or to protect our rights or the rights of others. Business transfers may occur if we're involved in a merger or acquisition.

4. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

We use cookies and similar tracking technologies to track activity on our website and hold certain information. Cookies are files with small amounts of data which may include an anonymous unique identifier. You can instruct your browser to refuse all cookies or indicate when a cookie is being sent, but this may affect some functionality of our services.

5. Data Security

We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect the security of your personal information including encryption, access controls, secure servers, and regular security assessments. However, no method of transmission over the Internet or electronic storage is 100% secure, so we cannot guarantee absolute security.

6. Data Retention

We retain your personal information only for as long as necessary to provide you with our services and as described in this Privacy Policy. Retention periods vary depending on the type of data and purpose for collection. Accounting records are typically kept for 7 years to comply with tax laws, while marketing data may be retained shorter periods.

7. Your Data Protection Rights

Depending on your location, you may have rights including: the right to access your personal data; the right to correct inaccurate information; the right to request deletion of your data; the right to restrict processing; the right to data portability; the right to object to processing; and rights related to automated decision-making. To exercise these rights, please contact us.

8. International Data Transfers

Your information, including personal data, may be transferred to and maintained on computers located outside of your country where data protection laws may differ. We take steps to ensure your data is treated securely and in accordance with this Privacy Policy, including implementing Standard Contractual Clauses where appropriate.

9. Children's Privacy

Our services are not directed to individuals under 13 (or other age as required by local law). We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If we become aware that a child has provided us with personal information, we will take steps to delete such information. Parents who believe their child has submitted personal data may contact us.

10. Third-Party Websites

Our website may contain links to third-party websites that are not operated by us. If you click on a third-party link, you will be directed to that third party's site. We strongly advise you to review the Privacy Policy of every site you visit. We have no control over and assume no responsibility for third-party sites or services.

11. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update our Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify you of any changes by posting the new Privacy Policy on this page and updating the "Last Updated" date. You are advised to review this Privacy Policy periodically for changes. Changes are effective when posted unless otherwise indicated.

12. Contact Us

If you have any questions about this Privacy Policy or our privacy practices, please contact us via email at privacy@dropleafpro.com or by mail at our physical address. We have appointed a Data Protection Officer who can be reached directly at dpo@dropleafpro.com for privacy-specific concerns.

13. Data Protection Officer

We have appointed a Data Protection Officer (DPO) to oversee compliance with this privacy policy. If you have any questions about how we process your personal data or requests regarding your personal data, you may contact our DPO at dpo@dropleafpro.com or through our postal address marked "Data Protection Officer."

14. California Privacy Rights

California residents have specific rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA). These include the right to know what personal information is collected, the right to delete personal information, the right to opt-out of the sale of personal information, and the right to non-discrimination for exercising these rights. California residents may make requests via our website or by contacting us.

15. GDPR Compliance

For users in the European Economic Area (EEA), we comply with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR). This includes implementing appropriate technical and organizational measures, ensuring lawfulness of processing through consent or other legal bases, honoring data subject rights, and maintaining records of processing activities. Our Data Processing Addendum is available upon request.

16. Marketing Communications

We may use your personal information to contact you with newsletters, marketing or promotional materials, and other information that may be of interest to you. You may opt-out of receiving these communications by following the unsubscribe link or instructions provided in any email we send or by contacting us. Even if you opt-out, you may still receive transactional messages.

17. User Accounts and Registration

When you create an account with us, you must provide accurate and complete information. You are responsible for maintaining the confidentiality of your account credentials and for all activities that occur under your account. We reserve the right to refuse service, terminate accounts, or remove content at our sole discretion.

18. Payment Information

All payment information is processed by our third-party payment processors and is not stored on our servers. We collect payment method details (such as credit card type and last four digits) for verification purposes and to facilitate future purchases. For recurring payments, we store payment tokens provided by our payment processor.

19. Sensitive Personal Information

We do not collect sensitive personal information such as racial or ethnic origin, political opinions, religious or philosophical beliefs, trade union membership, genetic data, biometric data, health data, or data concerning sexual orientation unless required by law or with your explicit consent for specific purposes.

20. Automated Decision-Making

We may use automated processing of personal data to provide certain features of our service, such as personalized recommendations. These decisions are made based on algorithms analyzing your usage patterns. You may have the right not to be subject to purely automated decisions that significantly affect you, with certain exceptions permitted by law.

21. Data Minimization

We adhere to the principle of data minimization, collecting only the personal information that is adequate, relevant, and limited to what is necessary for the purposes for which they are processed. When possible, we use anonymized or pseudonymized data that cannot reasonably be used to identify any individual user.

22. Data Accuracy

We take reasonable steps to ensure that personal data we process is accurate and kept up-to-date. We implement processes to periodically review and update our records. Users can review and update their account information by logging into their account settings or by contacting our customer support team.

23. Data Protection Impact Assessments

Where processing is likely to result in a high risk to individuals' rights and freedoms, we conduct Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIAs) to identify risks and implement mitigating measures. We consult with relevant supervisory authorities when required by applicable data protection laws.

24. Employee Data Privacy

We maintain strict privacy policies regarding employee and contractor data. Access to employee information is limited to personnel with a legitimate business need. Employees receive training on data protection and privacy best practices, and we implement appropriate security measures for employee records.

25. Vendor Management

We carefully vet third-party vendors who process personal data on our behalf to ensure they maintain adequate security measures. Contracts with vendors include data protection clauses as required by applicable laws. We regularly review vendor relationships and security practices to maintain privacy standards.

26. Data Breach Notification

In the event of a data breach involving personal data, we have procedures in place to contain, assess, and remediate the incident. We will notify affected individuals and relevant authorities as required by applicable laws, typically within 72 hours of becoming aware of a breach where required.

27. Location Data

Some features of our services may require collection of precise location data. You may enable or disable location services through your device settings or browser settings when using our website. We use location data to provide location-based services, improve our offerings, and analyze usage patterns.

28. Analytics and Performance Data

We use analytics services to help understand how users interact with our services. This data may include information like page views, feature usage, session duration, and navigation paths. We use this information to improve functionality, user experience, and content relevance while protecting individuals' privacy.

29. Social Media Integration

Our services may include social media features or widgets that may collect information about your IP address and which page you're visiting. Your interactions with these features are governed by the privacy policy of the social media company providing them. We encourage you to review these policies.

30. Data Retention for Marketing Purposes

We may retain your personal information for marketing purposes as long as you have not opted out of receiving marketing communications. You can unsubscribe from our marketing emails at any time, and we will remove your information from our marketing lists. However, we may still retain certain information for record-keeping purposes or to comply with legal obligations.

31. User Rights Under CCPA

California residents have specific rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), including the right to request disclosure of the personal information we collect, the right to request deletion of their personal information, and the right to opt-out of the sale of their personal information. To exercise these rights, please contact us using the information provided in this policy.

32. User Rights Under GDPR

For users in the European Economic Area (EEA), you have rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), including the right to access, rectify, or erase your personal data, the right to restrict or object to processing, and the right to data portability. To exercise these rights, please contact us as outlined in this policy.

33. Opting Out of Targeted Advertising

You can opt-out of targeted advertising by adjusting your ad preferences on the platforms you use. Many advertising networks allow you to opt-out of personalized advertising through their settings. Please note that opting out does not mean you will no longer see ads; it simply means that the ads you see may be less relevant to you.

34. Data Portability

You have the right to request a copy of your personal data in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format. This allows you to transfer your data to another service provider if you choose. To request your data, please contact us using the information provided in this policy.

35. Right to Withdraw Consent

If we are processing your personal data based on your consent, you have the right to withdraw that consent at any time. Withdrawing consent will not affect the lawfulness of processing based on consent before its withdrawal. To withdraw consent, please contact us as outlined in this policy.

36. Changes to Your Personal Information

It is important that the personal information we hold about you is accurate and current. Please keep us informed if your personal information changes during your relationship with us. You can update your information by logging into your account or contacting us directly.

37. Data Protection Training

We provide regular training to our employees on data protection and privacy best practices. This training ensures that our staff understands their responsibilities regarding personal data and how to handle it securely. We also conduct periodic reviews of our data protection policies and practices.

38. Privacy by Design

We incorporate privacy considerations into our business processes and product development from the outset. This approach, known as "privacy by design," ensures that we consider privacy implications at every stage of our operations and implement measures to protect personal data proactively.

39. User Feedback and Complaints

If you have any feedback or complaints regarding our privacy practices, we encourage you to contact us. We take all feedback seriously and will investigate any complaints promptly. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority if you believe we have not complied with applicable data protection laws.
